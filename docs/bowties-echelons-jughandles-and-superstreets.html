Bowties, Echelons, Jughandles and Superstreets

Do you know what those terms are? After reading an interesting article in the SF Chronicle about driving directions, they pointed me at an awesome site showing diagrams of “Unconventional Intersections”. Don’t forget to use the dropdown to show all the different types!

With phasing like this:

One approach on both the arterial and intersecting cross-street are elevated on structure as they intersect, while the other approach halves intersect at-grade. The result is a symmetrical but offset pair of two-phase intersections separated by grade, both operated by two-phase signals as in the meeting of two one-way streets.

What’s not to love?
