Turning off Firefox’s fsync() behavior.

Firefox 3.x tends to like to fsync() its sqlite database frequently. (see here) and this can cause the browser to lock up periodically under high disk or CPU load.

There are several ways to mitigate this.

  1. Switch your local disk drive (Linux) to data=writeback by adding this to your /etc/fstab (see instructions elsewhere)
  2. Use FF’s about:config to set the preference “toolkit.storage.synchronous” to “0” (it’s an integer)

I’ve done #2, and it seems to work great.  I’ve yet to see corruption of my bookmarks / awosome bar and I’m seeing reduced freezes.
