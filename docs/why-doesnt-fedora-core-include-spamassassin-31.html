Why doesn’t Fedora Core include spamassassin 3.1?

I’ve been battling all the image spam thats been happening lately, and my current solution has been to look at the messages that make it through my spam filters, and boost the scores for those rules.

The weird part is that my Fedora Core 4 installation has spamassassin-3.0.6, and the latest is version 3.1. So, I’ve just been copying the 3.1 scores from the spamassassin tests page and putting those in /etc/mail/spamassassin/local.cf. Its worked pretty well, and cut down some of the spam. I did have to boost RECV_IN_XML up to 15 to catch a bunch more things, but I think thats fairly safe.

The real question is: Why doesn’t the Fedora team get spamassassin 3.1 into the distro?!
